The Approach

Knowledge as Signal

Instead of representing text as dense token embeddings — where every dimension is active and computationally expensive — NFR encodes information into sparse frequency-domain waveforms where most of the signal is silence.

Encode

Text is transformed into amplitude and phase values across frequency bins by a neural encoder that learns which frequencies carry information.

Transform

The frequency representation becomes a real waveform through inverse Fourier transform — an actual oscillating signal, not an abstract vector.

Compress

Energy minimization forces the encoder to concentrate information into fewer frequencies. The result: 99.5% of the waveform energy is eliminated while information is preserved.

Predict

A spectral predictor analyzes sequences of waveforms and predicts what comes next — proving that waveforms carry meaningful sequential structure.

Implications

Why This Matters

If knowledge can be represented as sparse waveforms instead of dense vectors, the implications for AI efficiency are profound.

⚡

Compute Efficiency

Sparse frequency representations mean fewer active dimensions. Fewer active dimensions mean dramatically less computation at every layer of a neural network.

🧠

Cross-Domain Discovery

When knowledge from different fields exists as waveforms, combining them through superposition could reveal relationships invisible in text.

📡

Frequency-Native AI

A model that operates entirely in frequency space — never seeing text, only waveforms — represents a fundamentally different approach to artificial intelligence.

🔋

Edge Deployment

Models operating on compressed waveform representations could run on devices where traditional large language models cannot.
