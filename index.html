The Problem & The Solution

Dense representations are computationally wasteful

Modern AI systems encode knowledge in dense, high-dimensional vectors where every dimension is active. This drives enormous compute costs at training and inference.

Current Approach

Dense Token Embeddings

Every token is represented as a high-dimensional vector with thousands of active dimensions. Every dimension participates in every computation.

  • Billions of active parameters per forward pass
  • Memory bandwidth is the bottleneck, not compute
  • Training costs doubling every 6-9 months
  • Inference requires dedicated GPU clusters
NFR Approach

Sparse Frequency Waveforms

Knowledge is encoded as frequency-domain waveforms where information concentrates into a small number of active frequency bins. The rest is silence.

  • 99.5% of representation energy eliminated
  • Information preserved with 99.2% fidelity
  • Sparse signals = sparse computation
  • Fundamentally less data to move and process

Applications

Where NFR Creates Value

Sparse frequency representations have implications across the entire AI stack — from training infrastructure to edge deployment.

⚡

Training Efficiency

Models trained on sparse waveform representations require dramatically less computation per layer. Sparse matrix operations are hardware-optimized on modern GPUs and TPUs.

💰

Inference Cost Reduction

Representations that are 99.5% sparser mean 99.5% less data moving through every layer at inference. At scale, this translates directly to infrastructure cost savings.

📡

Edge & On-Device AI

Compressed waveform representations enable model deployment on resource-constrained devices — mobile phones, embedded systems, IoT — where dense models cannot run.

🔬

Scientific Discovery

Knowledge from different domains encoded as waveforms can be combined through superposition. Interference patterns may reveal cross-domain relationships invisible in text.

🧠

Frequency-Native Models

Models operating entirely in frequency space — processing waveforms instead of tokens — represent a new computational paradigm with unique capabilities.

🔐

Compact Knowledge Storage

Sparse spectral representations offer a fundamentally more efficient way to store and retrieve encoded knowledge, with natural compression built into the representation.
